A cinematic soundtrack designed for movement & tension

“Mystic” lives in the space between ambient sound design, trip-hop shadows and subtle rhythmic pulses. The music was written to follow a character’s inner journey: doubt, fascination, fear, obsession — emotions that immediately translate to the body on stage.

The score moves through slow drones, delicate piano patterns, distant voices and sudden rises in intensity. Instead of filling every space, it leaves room for silence, breath and choreography, giving performers the freedom to build their own phrases and dynamics.

Influences include Portishead, minimal piano in the spirit of Erik Satie, atmospheric textures reminiscent of Future Sound of London, and the deep emotional drive found in liquid drum & bass.

Why “Mystic” works particularly well for contemporary dance

  • Built-in crescendos for physical tension and group sections.
  • Suspended, spacious parts ideal for floor work and slow exploration.
  • Subtle rhythmic phrases for travel, jumps and momentum.
  • A strong narrative arc that can support solo, duo or ensemble pieces.
  • A sound palette that feels modern yet intimate, easy to integrate into festivals and venues.

Many sections can be used as standalone cues — you can cut, loop or reorder parts of the soundtrack to match your choreographic structure.

Technical details

  • Approx. duration: 30–40 minutes (depending on final selection of cues).
  • Tempo range: mostly 70–90 BPM feel, with a few more energetic passages.
  • Sound palette: pads, piano, processed vocals, low-frequency pulses, noise textures.
  • Recommended use: psychological short films, contemporary dance, experimental video, installation.

About the composer

Vincent Bastille (Vincent Letort) is a French composer, producer and director based in Le Mans. His work combines electronic music, sound design and visual storytelling, with a strong connection to contemporary dance and cinema.

Over the years he has created soundtracks for stage pieces, short films and experimental projects, blending club culture, ambient research and narrative sound. “Mystic” is part of his recent focus on film scores and dance-oriented soundscapes.
