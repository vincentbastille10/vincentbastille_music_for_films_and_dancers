An ambient, liquid & piano-driven score

Inspired by the shifting reflections of a summer lake, “Le Lac” explores slow-moving ambient textures, deep basslines and delicate piano motifs. The soundtrack was composed to support a film where sound, memory and landscape are deeply connected.

The music moves from intimate, almost whispered sections to more expansive movements where liquid drum & bass pulses appear under the surface, giving choreographers and editors multiple layers of energy to work with.

References include Erik Satie for the piano’s clarity, Portishead and trip-hop culture for the grain and textures, and LTJ Bukem for the liquid, atmospheric feel of the rhythm sections.

About the film “Le Lac”

“Le Lac” is a medium-length film written and directed by Vincent Bastille, produced by Spectra Media Prod. Shot around a lake and in the region of Le Mans, it blends intimate drama, mystery and experimental sound work including hydrophones and natural reverberation.

The soundtrack was written in close dialogue with the images and locations, making it particularly suitable for projects exploring memory, water, disappearance, reflection and doubles.

About the composer

Vincent Bastille is a French DJ, composer and director. Drawing from house and drum & bass culture as well as classical and ambient influences, he creates soundtracks that sit between club, cinema and contemporary dance.
