How to choose the right soundtrack for your short film

A simple method to avoid spending weeks on YouTube or stock libraries:

  1. Define the emotional spine of your film in one or two words: tension, nostalgia, obsession, loss, tenderness, etc.
  2. Decide the role of music: should it lead the scene, support it quietly, or disappear most of the time and come back only at key moments?
  3. List the key scenes where music is essential: opening, turning point, climax, end credits, etc.
  4. Listen to full albums (not only isolated tracks) to feel how a musical universe can support your film from start to finish.
  5. Reach out early if you think a custom theme or specific cue might be needed.

Custom score vs. licensing existing tracks

You can either:

  • License existing tracks from Mystic, Le Lac or other releases.
  • Ask Vincent to compose a custom score (full soundtrack, a few original cues, or a hybrid solution using both).

In both cases, the goal is to keep the process simple, human and transparent.
